  The UNCRC names identity, family, and cultural rights at
  treaty level — hence Identity Continuity and Family Integrity
  score W:100%. But naming is not measuring. The Convention has
  no enforcement mechanism, no data collection requirement, no
  mandatory reporting on outcomes for separated children, and
  no provision for the child's own consent to adoption
  proceedings.

  The three most damning numbers:

  SELF-DETERMINATION — ORI 3.03, W:5%, OMITTED
  Highest omission risk in the document. The child has no
  standing to refuse, initiate, or contest adoption. Article 12
  gives them the right to be heard — not to decide. That gap
  is structural and deliberate.

  CONSENT — ORI 2.66, W:10%, BLIND SPOT
  Article 21(a) requires informed consent from "persons
  concerned" — read as birth parents and adopters. The adoptee
  is not a consenting party to their own adoption under the
  Convention's framework.

  AGENCY — ORI 2.33, W:10%, BLIND SPOT
  The child is the subject of proceedings throughout. Never
  the initiating party.

  The Delta of 39 sits one point below Critical. The UNCRC
  reports 65/100 to itself. The true score if every domain
  were fully visible is 26/100. That 39-point gap is what the
  Convention cannot see about itself — and what forced adoptees
  have been saying for fifty years.
