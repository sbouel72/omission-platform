A risk that exists but is not seen is an omission. This platform quantifies the structural gap between documented coverage and actual visibility — and turns that gap into an auditable score.

The Problem It Solves

Standard governance audits ask: "Do you have a policy for X?" That question has a fatal flaw. A policy that exists but is invisible to the people responsible for acting on it provides no real protection. The harm still happens. The liability still accumulates. The audit still passes.

OMISSION asks the harder question: even if the policy exists — how well can the people who need it actually see it? It measures this gap across eight governance domains and surfaces the difference as a quantified risk score, not a checkbox.

Start here — the smoke detector. A landlord who doesn't install a detector in the kitchen can claim a perfect fire safety record, because they never recorded a kitchen fire. The detector wasn't there to catch it. A governance framework works the same way: if a domain isn't being measured, its harms don't appear in the score. The framework looks healthy. The harm keeps accumulating.

The core concept is recall — fires caught divided by total fires that actually occurred, including the ones in the rooms you weren't watching. Most governance frameworks inflate their recall by quietly shrinking the denominator: they only score the domains they already measure, then report the result as a complete picture.

The measurement model — read Blind Spot first Blind Spot    = avg(H × (1 − W))  // harm currently invisible to the framework
Visibility    = avg(W)             // share of harm landscape being measured
Risk Delta    = True Score − Current Score  // the gap — your hidden liability

H is harm potential (0–1): how severely this domain damages people or the organisation if it fails. W is visibility (0.01–1): how clearly the framework currently sees and measures this domain. The Blind Spot is the number that matters — harm your framework cannot see. A high Current Score alongside a large Blind Spot is the most dangerous result: the framework believes it is well-governed while the actual exposure is large.

The Eight Domains

Every assessment scores across the same eight governance areas:

Identity Continuity Family Integrity Cultural Continuity Information Rights Agency Consent Self-Determination Memory Preservation

Each domain gets its own H (harm potential) and W (visibility) rating. A domain with high harm potential and low visibility is your most urgent blind spot — regardless of whether your policy documentation for it looks complete on paper. The domain names in the platform match these exactly.

What You Get Out

Primary verdict Blind Spot The harm your framework cannot currently see. This is the number that drives the verdict — not the headline score.
Output Visibility Score What share of your harm landscape is actively measured. Higher is genuinely better here.
Output Omission Risk Index Penalises domains where harm is high and visibility is near-zero. One invisible high-harm domain dominates this number.
Output Structural Signature Which known governance pattern your visibility fingerprint most closely resembles — and why that matters.

How To Use It

There are two modes. Start with Quick Assessment. Use Deep Scan when you have documents.

  1. Open the tool No account, no installation, no data sent anywhere. Everything runs in your browser. Open the live demo and you are already inside the platform.
  2. Choose Quick Assessment (~5 minutes) Rate each of the eight domains yourself across two questions: H (harm potential — how severe is the worst-case impact if this domain is ignored?) and W (visibility — how clearly does your framework currently measure this?). The Blind Spot calculates live as you answer. No account or API key needed.
  3. Or run Deep Scan with your documents (~2 minutes) Upload governance documents — policies, frameworks, board reports. An AI model reads them and returns evidence-based H and W scores per domain, with a short evidence quote and confidence rating for each. Supports Claude (Anthropic), GPT-4.1 (OpenAI), and Gemini (Google). You supply your own API key — it is never sent to this server.
  4. Read the Blind Spot — not the headline score A high Current Score sounds good. It isn't necessarily. If the Blind Spot is also high, your framework appears healthy precisely because it cannot see the harm. That gap between what you report and what is actually at risk is the omission. The Blind Spot number is your real verdict.
  5. Prioritise by harm-weighted visibility Domains with high H and low W are your action list. Not the domains with the most documentation — the domains where the stakes are highest and the sight lines are shortest.
Deep Scan requires an API key. You can use Claude (Anthropic), GPT-4.1 (OpenAI), or Gemini (Google). Keys are held in browser memory only and never sent to this server. Get a key at console.anthropic.com, platform.openai.com, or aistudio.google.com. If running locally, Claude requires a local server due to CORS — run python3 -m http.server 8080 and open localhost:8080/omission-platform.html.

Who It Is For

OMISSION is designed for anyone who needs to answer the question a standard audit cannot: not whether governance exists, but whether it is seen. That includes governance leads running self-assessments, board members stress-testing what leadership actually has visibility over, researchers studying the structural gap between policy documentation and decision-maker awareness, and anyone preparing evidence for an accountability review.

It is not a compliance tool. It does not tell you whether your policies meet a regulatory standard. It tells you whether the people responsible for acting on those policies can see them clearly enough to act. That is a different and more difficult question.
